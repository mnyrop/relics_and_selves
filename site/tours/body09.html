The royal body





Joaquim Insley Pacheco, A Imperattriz Teresa Cristina


The imperial family -Pedro's wife, Empress Teresa Cristina, the princesses Isabel and Leopoldina, and the princes Pedro and Augusto- provided the secondary characters in this monarchical embodiment in which the state -rather than the nation- was depicted as a family, thus conveying a sense of dynastic continuity to the imperial regime. However, as this photograph by Insely Pacheco also shows, towards the end of his reign the Emperor and his family would adopt a more and more 'civilian' image, posing in bourgeois costume rather than the lavish 'aristocratic' attire they had adopted earlier on.
