Colonial beginnings





Claude Gay, Una tertulia en 1790

Even earlier, the French naturalist and polymath Claude Gay, who had been contracted by the conservative government in 1830 to travel through the country to survey and classify its geography, fauna and flora, but also to capture and catalogue its social forms and customs, had included in his 'Album of the Chilean Republic', published in 1854, a series of depictions of colonial sociability and the way they had been maintained and transformed since independence.
