The monumental other





Alfred P. Roll, La Agricultura, 1889

The Argentine pavilion at the 1889 world´s fair, meanwhile, only contained few elliptic references to the popular classes – as a laborious, rural working population with no ethnic or cultural disctinctives that would have made them different from an ideal image of the European rural worker. And this, indeed, seems to have been the intention here: to bury all remembrances of the turbulent past of indigenous malones and gaucho insurgencies under the image of a landscape cleansed for progress and civilisation.
