The popular other



  José S. Alvarez (Fray Mocho), José Vásquez, or Bautista Lignol alias The Galician 

Meanwhile, in the large and increasingly massified coastal cities, the growth of popular masses at the same time as social mobility remained stymied by oligarchic rule, increased a fear of the "dangerous classes". Disciplines such as sociology, social psychology and criminology, which had emerged shortly before in France, Italy, and Germany, rapidly spread among Latin American intelligentsias, as providing a language that permitted to articulate the rising sense of social unrest at the same time as containing it within a medicalizing perspective that made it possible to all but omit the social causes of the problem. Ironically, it were often the immigrant populations recently arrived from Europe in order to populate and "civilize" the emergent nation-states of Latin America who now became the focus of a racist and pathologizing gaze.
