Bourgeois habitus





Porcelain portraits, 1901


Upper-class women, by contrast, largely remained confined to the status of domestic goddesses, objects of social prestige to be paraded at dinner-parties and social outings to parks and theatres (described by the Argentine novelist Eugenio Cambaceres as rituals of arranging socially convenient marriages). This exhibit of 'Chilean women' by porcelain manufacturer Carlos Oswald, shown at the Pan-American Exhibition of 1901, sums up the status of the female body as a precious domestic commodity (significantly, however, only the face, the socially representative part of the body, is 'exposed' here for public showing abroad – the body itself remains, so to speak, in the house).
