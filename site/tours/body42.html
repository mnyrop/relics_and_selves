The romantic body





Aurélio de Figueiredo e Melo, Indio do Rio Walpes, 1882

At the Anthropological Exhibition of 1882, the romantic and the physiological gaze were invited simultaneously by the paintings of Indians by Décio Villares and Aurélio de Figueiredo, which had been commissioned by the curators of the exhibition. While Décio´s small-size portraits experimented with a physiognomic realism that sought to highlight the degree of physical evolution attained by different 'indigenous types', Aurélio´s life-size porrait of a Walpes Indian, profusely adorned with feathers and pearls, recalls the stage designs for Carlos Gomes´s opera adaptation of José de Alencar´s romantic adventure novel O Guarani, which had premiered at Milan in 1870 and travelled on to Rio de Janeiro in the same year.
