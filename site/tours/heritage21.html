Indigenous legacies





Ornaments, Museo de La Plata

Earlier on, new strands of anthropological writing and exhibitionary aesthetics had already started to develop a discourse on the legacies of preconquest aboriginal cultures, such as, for example, at the Museo de La Plata, re-founded in 1888 in a massive new gallery building that attempted to blend a neoclassical exterior with a mock-panamericanist decoration of the internal showrooms and stairways. Motives for these ornaments had been drawn not just from native pottery of the Argentine northwest, but also from Peruvian and Mexican monuments, suggesting that Argentina was amongst may other things also maintaining its spiritual ties with the old indigenous civilisations of the continent.
