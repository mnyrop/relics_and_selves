The body at war





Juan Manuel Blanes, Ocupación militar del Río Negro

Juan Manuel Blanes´s 'Military Occupation of the Río Negro under the Orders of General Julio A. Roca' (1894), a giant canvas of 3.55 x 7.10 metres also commissioned by the National Historical Museum to commemorate the anniversary of Roca´s 'Desert Campaign', was another monumental celebration of the 'heroic foundation of the state', this time with reference to the violent occupation of the southern pampas and Patagonia. The painting centers on a depiction of Roca himself and his general staff, an image of a civilising violence that exemplifies how the new ruling elite liked to see itself.
