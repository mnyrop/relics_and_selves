Flumen Junius, Folia do Divino

In Brazil, popular culture similarly began to be studied precisely by those concerned with the country's socio-ethnic composition, such as the Bahian physician Raimundo Nina Rodrigues, the first academic scholar of Afro-Brazilian culture but also the author of phrenological and forensic studies on the 'moral inferiority of Negroes' and its legal consquences, or Sylvio Romero, the positivist literary critic from Recife who whas also a collector and anthologist of popular verse and music. Alexandre José Mello Morães Filho, a historian who had also edited the journal of Rio de Janeiro's Anthropological Exhibition of 1882, collected memories of popular feasts from throughout the country.
