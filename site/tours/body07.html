The royal body





Pedro Américo de Figueiredo e Melo, D. Pedro na abertura da Assembléia Geral


The royal body itself was a complex mise-en-scène of this notion of a tropical empire, as Pedro Américo de Figueiredo e Melo's (one of the court's favourite painters) iconic portrait of the Emperor at the opening of the General Assembly shows: Pedro's medievalist attire is counterpointed by the 'tropicalist' elements of the cape of tucano feathers he wears around his neck, and the coat with ornamental tobacco and palm leaves, held in the royal colours green and yellow.
