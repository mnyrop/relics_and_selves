Marc Ferrez, Exposição Antropológica Brasileira, 1882

At the Brazilian Anthropological Exhibition of 1882, held at the National Museum of Rio de Janeiro, families of Cherente and Botocudo Indians from the interior were likewise put on display for the excitement of an urban public. The French sculptor Léon Deprès was commissioned to produce plaster replicas of them, which were used to arrange diorama-style displays of native villages and hunting scenes.
