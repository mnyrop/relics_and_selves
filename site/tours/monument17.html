The monumental city





Album de Santa Lucía, 1874

Following Vicuña Mackenna´s urban reforms of the early 1870s, Santiago de Chile had similarly acquired characteristics of a monumental urban landscape, where the remembrabce of the colonial city at the same time signalled its having been superseded by the modern, progressive city that commemorated it. This photograph of the top of Santa Lucía hill, where the city´s dignataries are posing atop the new landmark, with small vignettes of the 'views' that offer themselves up to the occupier of the platform, can thus also be read as a monumental representation of the 'patrician city' these men sought to forge.
