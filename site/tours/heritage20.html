Colonial beginnings





José Bouchet, La primera misa en Buenos Aires

A few years later, José Bouchet´s 'First mass in Buenos Aires', commissioned by the National Historical Museum on occasion of the Argentine centenary of independence, explicitly (and rather contrafactually) foregrounded an erotic image of miscigenation; an unusual twist in a national iconography which had for almost a century prided itself for Argentina´s exceptionality within South America, due to its exclusively 'white' blend of inhabitants. Yet at the beginning of the twentieth century, cosmopolitism itself had become a potential threat to the integrity of 'the national', and the idea of an 'American' core of Argentineness was beginning to spread through writings such as Juan Agustín García´s La ciudad indiana and Ricardo Rojas´s Eurindia.
