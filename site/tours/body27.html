Popular culture





José Ferraz de Almeida Júnior, Caipira picando fumo

In Brazilian art of the 1890s, a naturalist school of painting of which José Ferraz de Almeida Júnior was the most productive exponent, turned its attention to the harsh realities of rural life, though less in a mood of social criticism than in an attempt to craft 'popular types' for a nation which, as the republican politician and brother of the president Alberto Salles put it in 1901, had countrymen but no citizens, folk but not a people, and a country but not a society.
