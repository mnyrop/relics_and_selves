The indigenous other



  Luis de Servi, Descuartizando un gliptodonte

The frescoes painted on the walls of the entrance hall of Argentina's Museo de La Plata, are characteristic of this latter perception of nomadic Amerindians as a human remnant from a bygone age. Little or nothing had changed, according to these images, in the life of Patagonia's original inhabitants since the time of the great prehistoric mammals, now extinct. In fact, therefore, the state conquest of Patagonia and Araucania, taking place whilst the museum was being constructed and its collections assembled, was suggested to be but a belated fulfillment of evolution's own mandate. The "prehistoric people" of Patagonia were already museum pieces, human curiosities from another age that had overstayed their welcome in historical time.
