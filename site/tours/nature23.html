Carlos André Gomes, Rio Paraná

Previous iconographies of Brazil as a natural, 'virgin' space of tropical exuberance such as the image of the noble Indian, however, also continued to be invoked in the final decades of the century, as on Carlos André Gomes´s designs for the Brazilian pavilion at the Paris Universal Exhibition of 1889. Indigenous warriors of classical proportions and fantasy attire would be used to decorate the lateral sides of the construction, in allegorical representation of the four great rivers (Amazon, Paranaíba, Paraná and São Francisco) that bordered and connected national space.
