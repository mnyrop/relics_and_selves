The monumental city





Juan Gutiérrez, Arco de Triunfo, 1894

Not all the monuments that converted the capital city into a ceremonial space for the symbolic reproduction of political and social dominion were of a permanent nature, however. The improvements in the design of mock façades to conceal temporary steel armours, which were being used for the grandiloquent pavilions of national and universal exhibitions, also sparked the proliferation of ephemeral monuments such as the triumphal arch erected on occasion of Prudente de Morães´s presidential ascension in 1894.
