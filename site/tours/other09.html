The indigenous other



  Horacio Lara, Lautaro calling his people to arms


Whereas, in Mexico, Central America and the Andes, the final decades of the nineteenth century saw a re-appraisal of the legacy of pre-Columbian societies, as ruins and material remnants were being reclaimed as a cornerstone of national heritage, in Argentina, Brazil and Chile, with a much less sedentary or urbanized indigenous population prior to European conquest and colonization, the nation's indigenous ancestry was mostly only highlighted in an allegorical and legendary fashion, if at all. Such "acknowledgements" of indigenous roots actually removed Native Americans from national history proper, which was imagined as only having started with the arrival of the Europeans. The time of indigenous societies, by contrast, was literally pre-historical, a static and remote era beyond the dynamic time of history and progress.
