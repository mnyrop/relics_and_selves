Temples of science





Museo Nacional, sala principal

Santiago de Chile´s Museo Nacional, under the directorship of the German zoologist Rodolpho Philippi, similarly embodied state power as a repository of scientific knowledge of the territory and its contents. In 1906, the building itself suffered the impact of 'nature' when Santiago was struck by an earthquake, and several parts of the building had to be stabilised by supporting beams, as if once again allegorising the state edifice that was widely seen as suffering from rapid deterioration.
